# Giovanni de Micheli

**Lab:** Integrated Systems Laboratory (LSI)  
**EPFL profile:** [people.epfl.ch](https://people.epfl.ch/giovanni.demicheli)

Giovanni De Micheli is a pioneering Professor Emeritus at EPFL's Integrated Systems Laboratory (LSI) and Scientific Director of EcoCloud, specializing in Electronic Design Automation (EDA), logic synthesis, and design technologies for emerging computing platforms including superconducting, quantum, and nanosystems.

## Changelog

### 2026-07-10

- **[July 2025] Best Paper Award (SMACD 2025):** Paper *"SPFD-Based Delay Resynthesis"* (Costamagna, Meng, De Micheli) won Best Paper Award at the SMACD conference in Istanbul; introduces a don't-care-aware LUT decomposition technique for delay optimization targeting ASIC technologies.
- **[2025] IEEE Gustav Kirchhoff Award:** De Micheli is recipient of the prestigious 2025 IEEE Gustav Kirchhoff Award (announced on EPFL profile).
- **[2025] Active publication stream:** Multiple recent papers including *"Scalable Sequential Logic Synthesis Using Observability Don't Care Conditions"* (IEEE TCAD, July 2025), *"Cut Tracing with E-Graphs for Boolean FHE Circuit Synthesis"* (arXiv, June 2025), *"A New Perspective of Constructing Resource-Efficient Data-Lookup Quantum Oracles"* (IEEE QCE, Aug 2025), and *"Simulation-Guided Approximate Logic Synthesis Under the Maximum Error Constraint"* (IEEE TCAD, 2026 early access).
- **[2025] Approximate computing for deep learning:** New paper *"Gradient Estimation Methods of Approximate Multipliers for High-Accuracy Retraining of Deep Learning Models"* (arXiv, Sept 2025) extends earlier DATE 2025 work on approximate multiplier gradients for DNN retraining.
- **[2024] Test-of-Time Award (CODES+ISSS 2024):** Jointly with David Atienza for contributions to HW/SW codesign and system synthesis.
- **[2024] Best Student Paper Award (IWLS 2024):** *"Information Graph-Based Resubstitution For Networks of Look-Up Tables"* at IWLS 2024, Zurich.
- **Status:** Now Professor Emeritus (STI and IC schools) and Chief Scientific Officer/Scientific Director of EcoCloud at EPFL; LSI laboratory research continues actively under his direction.
