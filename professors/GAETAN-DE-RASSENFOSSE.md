# Gaétan de Rassenfosse

**Lab:** Chair of Science Technology and Innovation Policy (STIP)  
**EPFL profile:** [people.epfl.ch](https://people.epfl.ch/gaetan.derassenfosse)  
**Web:** [epfl.ch](https://www.epfl.ch/labs/stip/)  
**ORCID:** [0000-0002-7862-0918](https://orcid.org/0000-0002-7862-0918)  
**OpenAlex:** [A5086533373](https://openalex.org/A5086533373)  

Gaétan de Rassenfosse is an innovation economist at EPFL's STIP lab studying how legal and policy frameworks — especially patents and IP — shape technology creation and commercialization, using large-scale data, econometrics, and machine learning.

## Changelog

### 2026-07-10

- **New armasuisse grant (Jun 2026):** Prof. de Rassenfosse was awarded a grant by armasuisse for the project "Exploration of Agentic AI for Technology Monitoring," studying how Multi-Agent Systems (MAS) can autonomously track, analyze, and report on emerging technologies.
- **New paper in *Small Business Economics* (Jul 2026):** "Certification or cash prize: the heterogeneous effect of venture competitions" (with M. van den Heuvel) — uses Venture Kick data from ~1,000 Swiss startups to show that expert endorsement strongly boosts science-based startup survival (+41 pp over 2 years), while cash prizes mainly extend runway for low-cost ICT ventures.
- **New paper in *Journal of Empirical Legal Studies* (May 2026):** Study shows patent protection enables firms to charge 8–10% supra-competitive price premiums for consumer products — first direct empirical evidence of this kind.
- **Recent publications:** Active output across multiple venues including *Strategic Management Journal* ("Beyond the front page: in-text citations to patents," Oct 2025), *Research Policy* ("A new approach to measuring invention commercialization," Aug 2025), NBER ("The Effect of Application Fees on Entry into Patenting," Feb 2025), and several SSRN working papers on patent quality, FRAND licensing, and patent fees as innovation policy.
