# Serge Vaudenay

**Lab:** Security and Cryptography Laboratory (LASEC)  
**EPFL profile:** [people.epfl.ch](https://people.epfl.ch/serge.vaudenay)  
**Web:** [lasec.epfl.ch](https://lasec.epfl.ch/)  
**ORCID:** [0000-0001-9185-1449](https://orcid.org/0000-0001-9185-1449)  
**OpenAlex:** [A5073902849](https://openalex.org/A5073902849)  

Serge Vaudenay leads EPFL's Security and Cryptography Laboratory (LASEC), focusing on cryptographic analysis, post-quantum cryptography, lightweight cryptography, and secure communication channels.

## Changelog

### 2026-07-10

- **Initial profile created** for Serge Vaudenay (LASEC, EPFL).
- **Recent publications (2025–2026):** LASEC has been highly active, with papers at top venues including CRYPTO 2025 (PEGASIS on isogeny-based group actions; Verifiable Decapsulation for post-quantum KEMs), ASIACRYPT 2025 (Superglue: fast (2,2)-gluing isogenies), IACR Communications in Cryptology (multi-target security of PQ KEMs; FrodoKEM CCA-secure KEM; blind signatures from code equivalence), TCHES 2026 (floating-point leakage in Falcon's pre-image computation), and a blockchain workshop paper on Sponsored Fair Exchange (EUROCRYPT 2026 affiliated).
- **PhD completions:** Laurane Marco defended her PhD "Post-Quantum Cryptography beyond Encryption: Design and Cryptanalysis" (May 7, 2026); Abdullah Talayhan defended "Cryptography Under Counter-Intuitive Settings" (June 2025). Laurane Marco also received the Teaching Assistant Award 2025.
- **Active PhD students:** Keng-Yu Chen, Lewis Glabush, David Dervishi, Max Duparc, Augustine Picquet.
- **Research themes:** Post-quantum cryptography (isogenies, KEMs, code-based), side-channel attacks on PQ schemes (Falcon), anonymous tokens, blockchain-related cryptography, and hybrid encryption.
