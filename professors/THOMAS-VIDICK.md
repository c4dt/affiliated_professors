Thomas Vidick leads the Quantum Complexity and Cryptography Laboratory (QCC) at EPFL, working at the intersection of quantum computing theory, interactive proof systems, quantum error correction, and device-independent cryptography.

## Changelog

### 2026-07-10

- **New preprint (June 2026):** "Probabilistically Checking Quantum Proofs, with Interaction" (arXiv:2606.09588, with Baocheng Sun) — establishes a quantum interactive oracle proof (qIOP) for QMA, providing the first information-theoretically sound, local and robust characterization of QMA without requiring a quantum PCP theorem; the protocol exploits quantum locally testable codes (LTCs) combined with classical PCPP techniques.
- **Recent preprint (Jan 2026):** "Quantum Interactive Oracle Proofs" (arXiv:2601.12874) — also published in LNCS (Dec 2025), formalizing the quantum IOP model.
- **Recent preprint (Oct 2025):** "Derandomised tensor product gap amplification for quantum Hamiltonians" (arXiv:2510.01333).
- **Ongoing series (late 2024–2025):** Two-part work on the Aldous–Lyons Conjecture (arXiv:2408.00110 and arXiv:2501.00173), with Part II establishing undecidability.
- **FOCS 2024:** "Expansion of High-Dimensional Cubical Complexes with Application to Quantum Locally Testable Codes" (4 citations).
- Lab website minimal; only one PhD student (Aviv Taller) listed publicly.
