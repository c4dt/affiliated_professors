# Nate Foster

**Lab:** Networked Systems Abstractions Lab (LASER)  
**EPFL profile:** [people.epfl.ch](https://people.epfl.ch/nate.foster)  
**Web:** [laser.epfl.ch](https://laser.epfl.ch/)  
**ORCID:** [0000-0002-6557-684X](https://orcid.org/0000-0002-6557-684X)  
**OpenAlex:** [A5013378091](https://openalex.org/A5013378091)  

Nate Foster leads the Networked Systems Abstractions Lab (LASeR) at EPFL, developing programming languages and formal verification tools for networked systems, with flagship contributions to NetKAT, P4, and consistent network updates.

## Changelog

### 2026-07-10

- **New at EPFL**: Nate Foster's LASeR lab officially moved from Cornell to EPFL in January 2026.
- **PLDI 2026**: Paper accepted — *Weighted NetKAT: A Programming Language For Quantitative Network Verification* (extends NetKAT with quantitative/probabilistic reasoning).
- **ECOOP 2026**: Paper accepted — *NEST: Network Enforced Session Types* (type-theoretic enforcement of session protocols at the network level).
- **CAV 2026**: Paper accepted — *The Simulator's Blueprint: Automata Learning from Cybersecurity Logs*.
- **ACM Fellow (2025)**: Named ACM Fellow (class of 2024), announced January 2025.
- **ACM SIGCOMM Networking Systems Award (2025)**: P4 and Tofino selected as winner of the 2025 ACM SIGCOMM Networking Systems Award.
- **SOSP 2025**: Paper accepted — *The Design and Implementation of a Virtual Firmware Monitor*.
- **PLDI 2025**: Two papers — *StacKAT: Infinite State Network Verification* and *Active Learning of Symbolic NetKAT Automata* (both published June 2025).
- **CACM 2025**: *It Takes a Village: Bridging the Gaps between Current and Formal Specifications for Protocols* published in Communications of the ACM.
