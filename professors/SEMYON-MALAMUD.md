Semyon Malamud is an EPFL Associate Professor and Swiss Finance Institute chair whose research sits at the intersection of machine learning, high-dimensional statistics, and financial economics, with a focus on asset pricing, portfolio theory, and market microstructure.

## Changelog

### 2026-07-10

- **New NBER working paper (May/June 2026):** *Complex Modern Portfolio Theory* (with Hellum, Jensen & Kelly) — shows counterintuitively that Markowitz portfolio performance *improves* once the number of assets exceeds the number of observations (c > 1), rehabilitating MPT in high-complexity regimes.
- **New NBER working paper (May 2026):** *Scaling Point-in-Time Language Models* (NBER w35247) — extends Malamud's ML-in-finance agenda to large language model scaling for financial data.
- **New SSRN working paper (Jan 2026):** *Recursive Portfolio Machines* — further work on deep/recursive ML architectures for portfolio optimization.
- **New working paper (Dec 2025):** *Limits To (Machine) Learning* (with Chen & Kelly) — characterises a universal lower bound ("Limits-to-Learning Gap") on ML model fit, showing standard ML understates true predictability in financial data; 653 downloads, 2,098 abstract views.
- **New arXiv paper (May 2025):** *Training NTK to Generalize with KARE* (with Schwab, Kelly & Xu) — proposes a kernel adaptation method to improve generalisation of neural tangent kernel models.
- **Recent publication:** *An Intermediation-Based Model of Exchange Rates* (with Schrimpf & Zhang) published in *Review of Financial Studies*, Vol. 38, Issue 8 (2025).
- **Replication data released (Jun 2026):** Harvard Dataverse package for *Universal Portfolio Shrinkage*.
- PhD graduate: Boris Kuznetsov completed his thesis in 2025.
