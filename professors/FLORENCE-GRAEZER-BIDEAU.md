# Florence Graezer Bideau

**Lab:** Heritage, Anthropology and Technologies Research Group (HAT)  
**EPFL profile:** [people.epfl.ch](https://people.epfl.ch/florence.graezerbideau)  
**Web:** [epfl.ch](https://www.epfl.ch/labs/hat/fr/)  
**ORCID:** [0000-0002-9499-1479](https://orcid.org/0000-0002-9499-1479)  
**OpenAlex:** [A5010772457](https://openalex.org/A5010772457)  

Florence Graezer Bideau is an anthropologist and sinologist leading EPFL's HAT lab, researching heritage governance, cultural policy, memory, and technology transmission through comparative fieldwork across Asia, Europe, and Latin America.

## Changelog

### 2026-07-10

- **New edited volume (Routledge, March 2026):** *The Heritage, Creativity and Innovation Nexus: Critical Approaches and Interdisciplinary Perspectives* (co-edited with Peter Bille Larsen) published, with Graezer Bideau authoring the introductory chapter and a chapter on creativity and innovation in heritage bureaucracy (avalanche risk management as intangible cultural heritage in Switzerland).
- **New book (Bristol University Press, Sept 2025):** *The Digitalisation of Memory Practices in China*, including her chapter "Resistance of the Stone and Fragmented Digital Collective Memory in Gulou, China."
- **New book chapter (Oct 2025):** "Historic Urban Landscape in Beijing" published in a collected volume (Amsterdam UP, DOI: 10.5117/9789462983694/ch04).
- **Journal article (SociologieS, 2026):** "Hériter du patrimoine ou patrimonialiser l'héritage ? Du patrimoine à l'antipatrimoine" — theoretical contribution to the heritage/anti-heritage debate.
- **Journal articles (2024):** Two pieces in *Built Heritage* on industrial heritage reuse in the context of mega-events; article in *Anthropological Theory* on a critical anthropology of the (de)creative turn in heritage; article in *American Anthropologist* on heritage as new social engineering in China.
- **Active research project:** Avalanche risk management at UNESCO in the context of the climate crisis (HAT lab, ongoing).
- **HAT lab focus:** Two main research axes — (1) politics of heritage governance and practices, (2) anthropology of technologies and skill transmission.
