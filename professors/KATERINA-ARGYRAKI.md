Katerina Argyraki leads EPFL's Network Architecture Lab (NAL), focusing on network transparency, neutrality inference, and predictable software network functions, with current work spanning Internet latency mapping from gaming footage, edge caching as differentiation, and traffic localization.

## Changelog

### 2026-07-10

- **ACM SIGCOMM 2025 Best Student Paper Award**: PhD student Muhammad Abdullah (with Mughees Ur Rehman, Pavlos Nikolopoulos, and Argyraki) won for "Edge Caching as Differentiation," presented at SIGCOMM 2025 (August 2025) — studying how edge caching creates unintentional network differentiation.
- **Polysphère IC Teaching Award 2025**: Argyraki received this IC teaching award (by student vote) for a third time (previously 2019, 2022).
- **Associate Dean for Education**: Argyraki now also serves as Associate Dean for Education in the IC School (noted in EPFL people page), in addition to her research and teaching roles.
- **Recent publication**: "Flow/Path Performance Consistency" at IMC 2024 (November 2024).
- **Active GitHub repo**: `Advanced-Networking-Labs` was last pushed December 2025, supporting ongoing advanced networking coursework.
- **Tero project** (near-real-time Internet latency map from gaming footage) and **WeHeY** (traffic differentiation localization) remain active ongoing projects.
