# Boi Faltings

**Lab:** Artificial Intelligence Laboratory (LIA)  
**EPFL profile:** [people.epfl.ch](https://people.epfl.ch/boi.faltings)  
**Web:** [lia.epfl.ch](https://lia.epfl.ch/)  
**ORCID:** [0000-0002-7188-7230](https://orcid.org/0000-0002-7188-7230)  
**OpenAlex:** [A5060427576](https://openalex.org/A5060427576)  

Boi Faltings (Professor Emeritus, LIA closed July 2025) researched multi-agent systems, game-theoretic information elicitation, privacy-preserving AI, and continual learning, with recent post-retirement publications exploring evolutionary/developmental AI paradigms and LLM-based reasoning.

## Changelog

### 2026-07-20

- **Lab closure confirmed:** LIA officially closed at the end of July 2025 upon Prof. Faltings' retirement; no new students or projects are being taken on.
- **NAACL 2025 Outstanding Paper Award (May 2025):** "A Logical Fallacy-Informed Framework for Argument Generation" (Mouchel, Paul, Cui, West, Bosselut, Faltings) was one of only 10 papers selected for this distinction at NAACL 2025.
- **New publication – IEEE TETCI (Nov 2025):** "Foundations of a Developmental Design Paradigm for Integrated Continual Learning, Deliberative Behavior, and Comprehensibility" — proposes a bio-inspired developmental approach to AI architecture.
- **New arXiv preprint (Jun 2025):** "Evolutionary Developmental Biology Can Serve as the Conceptual Foundation for a New Design Paradigm in Artificial Intelligence" — extends the evo-devo AI theme.
- **GECCO 2025 paper (Jul 2025):** "On the Parallels Between Evolutionary Theory and the State of AI."
- **CopyJudge (Oct 2025):** "CopyJudge: Automated Copyright Infringement Identification and Mitigation in Text-to-Image Diffusion Models" — new application area (copyright in generative image models).
- **LLMs for Resource Allocation (Oct 2025):** "LLMs for Resource Allocation: A Participatory Budgeting Approach to Inferring Preferences" published in Frontiers in AI.
- Three final PhD students (Erden, Cui, Rokvic) completed their theses in 2025, closing out the lab's PhD programme.
