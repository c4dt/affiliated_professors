# Mario Paolone

**Lab:** Distributed Electrical Systems Laboratory (DESL)  
**EPFL profile:** [people.epfl.ch](https://people.epfl.ch/mario.paolone)  
**Web:** [desl-pwrs.epfl.ch](https://desl-pwrs.epfl.ch/)  
**ORCID:** [0000-0001-7073-9036](https://orcid.org/0000-0001-7073-9036)  
**OpenAlex:** [A5050504092](https://openalex.org/A5050504092)  

Mario Paolone leads EPFL's Distributed Electrical Systems Laboratory (DESL), focusing on smart grid technologies including real-time monitoring and control of active distribution networks, PMU-based situational awareness, optimal planning of distributed energy resources, and integration of renewables and storage in power systems.

## Changelog

### 2026-07-10

- **Initial profile created** (no prior profile existed).
- **ABB Award 2025**: PhD student Rahul Gupta (supervised by Paolone & Fabrizio Sossan) received the Asea Brown Boveri Ltd. ABB Award for his thesis *"Methods for Grid-aware Operation and Planning of Active Distribution Networks"*, praised as "a seminal contribution to the advancement of power systems engineering" (announced Oct 2025).
- **New GitHub repo – `cflowdist`**: A Python toolkit for tracing carbon flows and allocating carbon intensity in distribution grids, released ~Jul 2026; accompanies the journal paper "Carbon flow tracing in distribution grids: From method to application" (EPSR, Jun 2026).
- **High publication volume mid-2026**: Eight papers appeared in *Electric Power Systems Research* between late June and early July 2026, covering EV battery scheduling, multi-energy co-planning toward net-zero, stochastic OPF with polynomial chaos, synchrophasor estimation (DCSOGI), grey-box dynamic equivalents, and OPF-based network restoration.
- **New journal papers (2025–2026)**: Notable outputs include a two-stage convex framework for energy-storage siting/sizing in large-scale grids (SSRN preprint), grid-aware islanding/resynchronisation of AC/DC microgrids (IEEE Trans. Smart Grid), identification of power system dynamic model parameters via Fisher Information Matrix (IEEE Trans. Power Systems), and dispatchability of data-centre PV+storage (Sustainable Energy Grids & Networks).
- **New PhD completions (2025)**: Willem Lambrichts, Aldo Leonardo Alerci, Simone Rametti, and Shubham Sharad Bhoir all received EPFL PhDs under Paolone's supervision.
