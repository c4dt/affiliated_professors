# Carmela Troncoso

**Lab:** Security and Privacy Engineering Lab (SPRING)  
**EPFL profile:** [people.epfl.ch](https://people.epfl.ch/carmela.troncoso)  
**Web:** [spring.epfl.ch](https://spring.epfl.ch/)

**ORCID:** [0000-0002-2374-2248](https://orcid.org/0000-0002-2374-2248)

Carmela Troncoso leads the SPRING Lab (now spanning EPFL and MPI-SP) researching security and privacy engineering, with a focus on privacy-preserving systems, ML fairness/security, and privacy evaluation frameworks.

## Changelog

### 2026-07-10

- **New role (Feb 2025):** Carmela Troncoso is now a Scientific Director at the [Max Planck Institute on Security and Privacy (MPI-SP)](https://www.mpi-sp.org/carmela-troncoso), currently on leave from EPFL. The SPRING lab now spans two campuses in Switzerland and Germany.
- **USENIX Security 2025 Distinguished Paper:** "Characterizing and Detecting Propaganda-Spreading Accounts on Telegram" (Klim Kireev et al.) won the Distinguished Paper award at USENIX Security 2025.
- **USENIX Security 2025:** "Double-Edged Shield: On the Fingerprintability of Customized Ad Blockers" also appeared; artifacts released as [`spring-epfl/flfp`](https://github.com/spring-epfl/flfp).
- **IEEE S&P 2025 papers:** "403 Forbidden? Ethically Evaluating Broken Access Control in the Wild" and "On the Conflict Between Robustness and Learning in Collaborative Machine Learning".
- **IEEE S&P 2026:** "Evaluating Concept Filtering Defenses against Child Sexual Abuse Material Generation by Text-to-Image Models" accepted; dataset released as [`spring-epfl/iccwd`](https://github.com/spring-epfl/iccwd).
- **Recent publications (2025–2026):** "On the (Privacy) Harms of the European Digital Identity Framework" (CISPA preprint); "Humanitarian Aid Distribution with Privacy-Preserving Assessment Capabilities" (PoPETs 2026); "Website Fingerprinting on Nym: Attacks and Defenses" (PoPETs 2026); "End-to-End Encrypted Collaborative Documents based on Signal"; "Should I use Synthetic Data for That?" (arXiv).
- **Active GitHub repos:** [`AgentLab`](https://github.com/spring-epfl/AgentLab) and [`BrowserGym`](https://github.com/spring-epfl/BrowserGym) — open-source frameworks for web agent development and benchmarking, recently active (March 2026).
- **PhD completions (2024–2025):** Theresa Stadler (2024) and Klim Kireev (2025) completed their PhDs.
