Andrea Cavallaro leads the MINTS lab at EPFL, researching multimodal intelligent systems spanning privacy-preserving machine learning, AI safety and alignment, robustness/interpretability, and multi-modal perception for autonomous systems.

## Changelog

### 2026-07-10

- Several new publications since last profile (sources were previously unavailable):
  - **PrivLEX** (arXiv, Jan 2026): VLM-based detection of legal/GDPR privacy concepts in images.
  - **Which private attributes do VLMs agree on and predict well?** (arXiv Feb 2026 + ICASSP 2026): benchmarking VLMs on private attribute detection vs. human observers.
  - **The Impact of Abstract and Object Tags on Image Privacy Classification** (ICASSP 2026): new work on automated image privacy tagging.
  - **MM-HSD: Multi-Modal Hate Speech Detection in Videos** (ACM MM, Oct 2025): fuses video frames, audio, text and cross-modal attention; already cited 3 times.
  - **Shortcut Flow Matching for Speech Enhancement** (ICASSP 2026): step-invariant flow matching for speech enhancement under drone noise.
  - **High-Resolution Open-Vocabulary Object 6D Pose Estimation** published in **IEEE TPAMI** (Oct 2025), the lab's flagship robotics perception result.
  - **Let's DENSE** (npj Robotics, Oct 2025): new protocol for tactile slip detection data collection in robotic grasping.
  - **NaviFormer** and **Language-Conditioned Robot Manipulation** presented at **IROS 2025**.
  - **Contextual Scalarisation Thompson Sampling for multi-objective decisions in public media** (arXiv, May 2026): new work on multi-objective bandit methods for public media content delivery.
  - IEEE Resource Center webinar on **Multi-Modal Sensing for Physical Human-Robot Interaction** published June 2026.
  - Cavallaro was named **ELLIS Fellow** (2025) — a notable new fellowship award.
  - Active 2025 keynote tour: ACM SAC (privacy in AI age), WAIC-S (trustworthy AI), ICIAP-W (VLMs for embodied AI), EUVIP (image privacy).
