Rüdiger Fahlenbrach is a Full Professor of Finance at EPFL and Director of the College of Management, specializing in empirical corporate finance with a focus on corporate governance, entrepreneurship, and the economics of startups and venture capital.

## Changelog

### 2026-07-10

- **New NBER Working Paper (May 2026):** "Organization Capital, Large Startups, and the Dearth of IPOs" (with Leandro Sanz and René M. Stulz) — proposes and tests a causal explanation for why organization-capital-intensive startups (relying on economies of scale and network effects via digital technologies) prefer to remain large private firms rather than IPO, using LinkedIn workforce data and the 2016 Defend Trade Secrets Act as a legal shock.
- **Working Paper (2024):** "Bank Payout Policy, Regulation, and Politics" released as NBER WP 32770 — examines post-crisis regulatory influences on bank dividend and buyback decisions.
- **Working Paper (2024):** "Why do startups become unicorns instead of going public?" circulated on SSRN — directly precedes and feeds into the 2026 NBER paper on organization capital.
- **Working Paper (2024):** "Navigating New Norms: The Response of LBO Financing to Post-Crisis Regulation and Market Shifts" (updated title of earlier LBO debt market paper) on SSRN.
- **Published (2023):** "Greening the Swiss National Bank's Portfolio" in *Review of Corporate Finance Studies* and "How Do Investors and Firms React to a Large, Unexpected Currency Appreciation Shock?" in the same journal.
- **Published (2023):** "CEO networks and the labor market for directors" in *Journal of Empirical Finance*.
- **Book chapter (2025):** "Unicorns" entry published in reference volume (Springer).
- **PhD student Daria Davydova completed thesis (2025)**; Luiz Ricardo Maldonado Bissoto completed thesis (2026).
- Fahlenbrach has served as Director of the College of Management (CDM) at EPFL since August 2022.
