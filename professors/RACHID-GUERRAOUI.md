Rachid Guerraoui leads EPFL's Distributed Computing Laboratory (DCL), pioneering research in robust (Byzantine-resilient) machine learning, distributed algorithms, secure distributed storage, and concurrent computing.

## Changelog

### 2026-07-10

- **ByzFL library (v0.0.11, Jul 2025):** The lab released and actively maintains `ByzFL`, an open-source Python library (pip-installable, 36 ⭐) for Byzantine-resilient federated learning, supporting robust aggregators, attack simulations, and full FL simulation pipelines (PyTorch & NumPy). Accompanied by an arXiv paper (González et al., 2025, arXiv:2505.24802).
- **Multiple 2025–2026 awards:** Guerraoui received the ACM Barroso Award, the Jean-Claude Laprie IEEE Award, Best Professor Award at EPFL IC, and was elected to Academia Europea — all in 2025. He also received the Dahl-Nygaard Prize (ECOOP 2024).
- **Recent papers (2025–2026):** Active publication stream including "Scalable Accountable Byzantine Agreement" (IEEE S&P 2026), "Your Neighbors Know: Backdoor Detection in Decentralized Learning" (arXiv, May 2026), "Distributional Machine Unlearning via Selective Data Removal" (arXiv, Jul 2025), "Certified Unlearning for Neural Networks" (arXiv, Jun 2025), "Stabl: The Sensitivity of Blockchains to Failures" (Dec 2025), and "Repeated Agreement is Cheap! On Weak Accountability and Multishot Byzantine Agreement" (Jun 2025).
- **SWARM system:** Fault-tolerant RDMA-based disaggregated key-value store published at SOSP 2024 with open-source artifacts.
- **New PhD students (active):** Beatrice Shokry, Abdellah El Mrini, Cristian-Alexandru Botocan, Clément Burgelin, Diana Andreea Petrescu, Antoine Murat.
- **Several PhD students graduated in 2025:** Jovan Komatovic, Manuel Vidigueira, Sadegh Farhadkhani, Youssef Allouah, John Stephan.
- **CS453 & CS451 course repos updated** for 2025 academic year.
